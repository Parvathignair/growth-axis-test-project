Should you have dental implants?

Dental implants are one option for replacing missing or failing teeth. However, they are not always the most appropriate treatment for every patient.

At Lifestyle Smiles, the focus is on:

  • Determining clinical suitability
  • Explaining all available options
  • Providing a clear, individualised treatment plan

This includes identifying cases where:

  • Implants may require additional procedures
  • Alternative treatments may be more appropriate
  • Treatment may be staged over time

Treatment Options

Options discussed based on your situation

Depending on clinical findings, a range of treatments may be considered — not just implants. The right solution depends on your bone health, oral condition, lifestyle, and long-term goals.

01
Dental Implants

Titanium posts placed in the jawbone to support a crown, bridge, or full-arch restoration. A long-term solution that preserves bone and neighbouring teeth.

02
Implant-Supported Restorations

Bridges or prostheses anchored to implants — often suitable for multiple missing teeth with fewer individual placements required.

03
Bridges

Fixed restorations spanning a gap, supported by adjacent natural teeth. A non-surgical alternative where teeth on either side are present and healthy.

04
Removable Dentures

Full or partial dentures for patients where implants are not yet appropriate, or where a simpler solution is preferred.

05
Monitoring or Staged Treatment

In some cases, the best course is to delay or phase treatment over time — clinically indicated to improve long-term outcomes.

Each option is evaluated for
  • Clinical suitability — bone quality, existing dentition, medical history
  • Longevity — expected lifespan of the restoration and long-term prognosis
  • Maintenance — what ongoing care each option requires over time
  • Staged timing — whether treatment is better spread across appointments
  • Cost implications — transparent comparison of each pathway
